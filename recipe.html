Porridge Beans Recipe

Nigerian Porridge beans, also known as beans Porridge , is a delicious, hearty dish made with beans simmered in a rich pepper sauce and proteins. Beans naturally is nutritious, filling, and can be made into serveral dishes, fitting perfectly in every occassion. sometimes misjudged as ewa agoyin when mixed with the sauce, it can also be paired with bread, yam, spaghetti, garri, pap, etc.

Recipe

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 1 hour:20 minutes

Servings: 4-6 people

Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredients

  • 2 cups brown beans (black-eyed peas or oloye beans)
  • 1 and 1/2 serving spoon of palm oil
  • 2 medium sized onions, blended
  • 2 red bell peppers, blended
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • 3 teaspoons ground crayfish
  • Salt to taste
  • 2-3 cups water (as needed)
  • stockfish (optional)
  • 1/4 cup diced yam (optional)
  • 1/2cup diced plantain (optional)
  • 1 dried fish (optional)

Directions

  1. Wash and soak the beans for about 30 minutes to soften them.
  2. Place the beans in a pot, add water, and cook until tender.
  3. Wash put the diced yam to cook for about 10minutes to soften them. At this point, feel free to include the palm oil and stockfish
  4. After it has softened, add the dried fish
  5. Add blended onions, peppers, and crayfish and plantain. Cook on low heat for 10-15 minutes.
  6. Add seasoning cubes and salt and leave to cook for 2 minutes and stir
  7. Allow to simmer on low heat for 5â€“10 minutes until the beans is well mixed and creamy.
  8. Add the chopped onions, stir and leave to cook for additional 2 minutes then turn off the cooker.
  9. Serve hot with fried plantains, bread, or rice.
  10. Serve hot with fried plantains, bread, or rice.

Tips

For a richer taste, you can add ugwu leaves. If the beans get too thick, add a little hot water to loosen it. You can also fry some plantain to add on the side if you dont want ot mixed with the plaintain during the cook time.
